Graham Charles MacKinnon was born in Bridgetown, Western Australia, on December 10, 1916.

Career

MacKinnon’s political career began in 1956 when he was elected to the two-member South-West Province in the Legislative Council of Western Australia, replacing the retiring Les Craig.

He quickly impacted and was appointed deputy chairman of committees in 1958.

MacKinnon’s dedication and leadership led to his appointment as a minister in the government of David Brand, where he served as Minister for Health and Minister for Fisheries and Fauna.

His passion for environmental protection and education was recognized, and he subsequently held positions such as Minister for Environmental Protection, Minister for Education, Minister for Cultural Affairs, Minister for Recreation, and Minister for Tourism throughout his tenure.
