Brooks Brothers, the iconic American luxury fashion brand, has a rich history that dates back to its founding in 1818 by Henry Sands Brooks. As the oldest apparel brand in continuous operation in the United States, Brooks Brothers has been a prominent name in the fashion industry for over two centuries.

Originally a family business, Brooks Brothers has evolved into a renowned company that produces clothing for men, women, and children, as well as home furnishings. Known for its classic and timeless designs, the brand has left an indelible mark on American style and menswear.

In September 2020, Brooks Brothers underwent a change in ownership. The company was purchased by a joint venture between Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group, leading to a new era for the brand. Currently, Brooks Brothers is owned by Authentic Brands Group and SPARC Group LLC.

Brooks Brothers’ Early History and Founding

In 1818, Henry Sands Brooks founded Brooks Brothers, originally named H. & D. H. Brooks & Co. Starting as a small family business, the company focused on providing quality clothing for men. In 1850, Henry’s sons renamed the company as Brooks Brothers, marking the beginning of its iconic journey.

One notable milestone in the early history of Brooks Brothers was the adoption of the Golden Fleece symbol as its trademark in 1850. This symbol, representing a wooly sheep suspended in a ribbon, was not only a symbol of British woolen merchants, but it also served as the emblem of the Knights of the Golden Fleece.

Brooks Brothers played a significant role in revolutionizing American fashion with the introduction of ready-to-wear suits in the mid-19th century. This innovation allowed customers to purchase suits without the need for extensive tailoring.

“Clothes may make the man, but the suit makes the gentleman,”
highlighting the impact of Brooks Brothers on accessible and stylish menswear.

Brooks Brothers’ reputation for exceptional craftsmanship also led to prestigious clientele. The company outfitted Abraham Lincoln with a unique suit for his second inauguration, and tragically, he was wearing a Brooks Brothers suit at the time of his assassination.

Ownership Changes and Expansion in the 20th Century

In the 20th century, Brooks Brothers experienced a series of ownership changes and underwent expansion to further establish its presence in the fashion industry. These changes shaped the trajectory of the brand, contributing to its growth and evolution.

Ownership Changes

In 1946, the Brooks family, who had been at the helm of the company since its inception, sold Brooks Brothers to Julius Garfinckel & Co., a prominent retail corporation. This transition marked a significant shift in ownership, as the brand entered a new era under the management of Julius Garfinckel & Co.

“The involvement of Julius Garfinckel & Co. brought fresh perspectives and opportunities for Brooks Brothers, paving the way for future expansion and recognition.”

Throughout the following decades, Brooks Brothers continued to navigate changes in ownership. In 1988, the brand was acquired by the renowned retailer Marks & Spencer, further solidifying its position in the global fashion market.

Introduction of “B by Brooks Brothers”

During its history, Brooks Brothers introduced the sub-brand “B by Brooks Brothers,” offering a distinct line of products that catered to a broader customer base. This sub-brand was strategically designed to provide an affordable yet high-quality option for customers seeking Brooks Brothers’ distinctive style.

“‘B by Brooks Brothers’ showcased the brand’s commitment to accessibility while maintaining its reputation for craftsmanship and quality.”

With an exclusive partnership with Macy’s, “B by Brooks Brothers” served as a gateway for more individuals to experience the timeless elegance that the brand embodies.

Recent Financial Struggles and Acquisition

In recent years, Brooks Brothers faced financial challenges, culminating in the company filing for bankruptcy protection in July 2020. However, the brand’s reputation and historical significance attracted the attention of Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group.

These two major players in the industry recognized the value of Brooks Brothers and, in September 2020, acquired the company, pledging to continue its operations and preserve its retail locations.

The acquisition by Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group reaffirms their belief in the enduring legacy and potential of the Brooks Brothers brand.

Ownership and Operations of Brooks Brothers

Brooks Brothers, the iconic American fashion brand, is currently owned by Authentic Brands Group and SPARC Group LLC. Authentic Brands Group is responsible for the brand’s ownership and licensing, while SPARC Group handles the day-to-day operations of the company.

Leading the charge as the CEO of Brooks Brothers is Ken Ohashi, a seasoned executive with an extensive background in the retail industry. Ken Ohashi brings a wealth of experience and strategic vision to guide the brand’s operations.

Ensuring that the creative direction stays true to the brand’s heritage is Michael Bastian, the renowned fashion designer and creative director of Brooks Brothers. With his keen eye for style and attention to detail, Michael Bastian continues to elevate the brand’s aesthetic and keep it at the forefront of American fashion.

In addition to its ownership and operations, Brooks Brothers has entered into licensing partnerships with industry leaders. The brand has collaborated with Luxottica, the Italian eyewear company, to create a collection of timeless and sophisticated eyewear. Fragrance enthusiasts can enjoy Brooks Brothers’ signature scents through a licensing partnership with Interparfums, a Paris-based fragrance company. Furthermore, Brooks Brothers has partnered with Turko Textiles to bring its refined and elegant style to the realm of home furnishings.

Overall, the ownership and operations of Brooks Brothers are meticulously curated to uphold the brand’s legacy of quality, craftsmanship, and timeless style.

Manufacturing and Production of Brooks Brothers Products

Brooks Brothers takes pride in its commitment to manufacturing and production, emphasizing quality craftsmanship and the use of elevated fabrications. While not all fabrics are produced in the United States, the company maintains a significant manufacturing presence in the country, particularly in the assembly of fabrics and the production of certain products.

The Golden Fleece line of suits, renowned for their exceptional quality and attention to detail, are manufactured in the United States. The 1818 line suits, celebrated for their timeless style, are also produced domestically. Additionally, ties are cut and piled in Long Island, ensuring meticulous craftsmanship. Many shirts, known for their impeccable tailoring, are primarily made in North Carolina.

This focus on manufacturing in the United States allows Brooks Brothers to maintain a high level of quality control and uphold their reputation for excellence. The brand’s commitment to skilled craftsmanship ensures that every garment is constructed with precision and care.

Brooks Brothers understands the importance of adapting to changing manufacturing practices while staying true to their heritage. By combining traditional techniques with modern innovations, they continue to deliver a diverse product range that meets the evolving demands of their customers.

Financial Challenges and Bankruptcy Protection

Brooks Brothers, like many other businesses, faced significant financial struggles as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. The company experienced store closures and witnessed a decline in online sales, creating a challenging environment for its operations. In July 2020, Brooks Brothers made the decision to file for bankruptcy protection to address its financial difficulties.

As part of the bankruptcy proceedings, the brand was acquired by Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group in September 2020. These new owners have expressed their commitment to keeping the business running and maintaining its retail locations. Although the company has faced challenges in recent years due to changing fashion trends and increased online competition, the acquisition provides a new opportunity for Brooks Brothers to overcome its financial struggles and regain stability.

The Impact of the COVID-19 Pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic had a profound effect on Brooks Brothers, as it did on numerous retailers around the world. With temporary store closures and reduced foot traffic, the company experienced a significant decline in sales. Additionally, the pandemic accelerated the shift towards online shopping, posing challenges for the brand’s traditional brick-and-mortar business model.

Brooks Brothers recognized the need to take decisive measures to ensure its survival in the face of these challenges, leading to the decision to file for bankruptcy protection. Despite these difficulties, the brand remains determined to adapt to the changing retail landscape and emerge stronger from this financial setback.

Continuing Operations and Maintaining Retail Locations

The acquisition of Brooks Brothers by Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group ensures the continuation of the brand’s operations and the preservation of its retail locations. This commitment demonstrates the new owners’ belief in the value and potential of the Brooks Brothers brand, despite its recent financial struggles.

By maintaining physical stores, Brooks Brothers can continue to provide customers with the opportunity to experience its products firsthand and receive personalized service. The brand recognizes the importance of these retail spaces in conveying its heritage, craftsmanship, and commitment to quality.

A Path to Financial Recovery

While overcoming the financial challenges and bankruptcy protection process will require careful planning and strategic decision-making, the acquisition by Authentic Brands Group and Simon Property Group offers a path to financial recovery for Brooks Brothers.

The new owners are expected to bring their expertise and resources to support the brand’s revitalization efforts. With a focused approach towards reinvigorating its retail presence, adapting to changing consumer demands, and leveraging its heritage and craftsmanship, Brooks Brothers aims to restore its financial stability and position itself for long-term success.
