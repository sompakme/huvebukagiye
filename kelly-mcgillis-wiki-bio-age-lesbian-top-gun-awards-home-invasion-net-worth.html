    Her film career debuted in Reuben, Reuben in 1983. She had her breakthrough in the 1985 film Witness with Harrison Ford in her role as an Amish mother, for which she received Golden Globe and BAFTA award nominations.

    Her other big role was that of flight instructor Charlie in the 1986 fighter-pilot film Top Gun with Tom Cruise and Val Kilmer. In 1987, McGillis acted in the fantasy-comedy film Made in Heaven, which also co-stars Oscar winner Timothy Hutton and was directed by Alan Rudolph, which was produced by Lorimar Productions.

    Kelly appeared in Cat Chaser with Peter Weller, a film she despised and which discouraged her from pursuing an acting career. From the late 1980s to the mid-1990s, she appeared in Winter People (1989), and North (1994), her 2nd Amish part in television or film, as well as several made-for-TV films.

    Kelly McGillis Stage Roles

    McGillis appeared in a couple of off-broadway and Broadway theater productions during the 1980s in New York City. Through the mid-2000s, she was a featured actress at the prestigious Shakespeare Theatre Company in Washington DC. She appeared in a production of John Webber’s play “The Duchess of Malfi” at the Shakespeare Theatre, Washington, DC in 2002.

    In 2004, McGillis appeared in the stage play The Graduate as Mrs. Robinson, touring the United States. In May 2009, McGillis starred in a Pasadena Playhouse stage production of The Little Foxes by Lillian Hellman, co-starring with Julia Duffy. She made an appearance in the production of Frankie and Johnny in the Clair de Lune by Terrence McNally, which toured the United Kingdom in 2010.

    She joined the cast of Showtime’s The L Word for its fifth season in 2007. In 2010, she starred alongside Nick Damici, Connor Paolo, and Danielle Harris in the vampire film Stake Land, directed by Jim Mickle. She starred in Ti West’s 2011 thriller The Innkeepers. McGillis was featured in a breast cancer docu-drama titled 1 a Minute, which was released in 2010. She also had a lead role in Mother of All Secrets in 2017 as Rose Lewis.
