Early Life and Career Beginnings

Martina Navratilova, born on October 18, 1956, in Prague, Czechoslovakia (now the Czech Republic), showed exceptional talent in tennis from a young age. At just 15 years old, she won the Czechoslovakia national tennis championship, showcasing her potential and setting the stage for an incredible career to come.

Navratilova’s early training and development were greatly influenced by her mother, who was an accomplished tennis player herself. Under her mother’s guidance, Navratilova honed her skills and continued to excel in the sport.

In 1972, at the age of 16, Navratilova made her debut on the United States Law Tennis Association, a stepping stone towards her eventual professional career. Three years later, in 1975, she made the decision to turn professional and embark on a journey that would solidify her status as one of the greatest female tennis players in history.
