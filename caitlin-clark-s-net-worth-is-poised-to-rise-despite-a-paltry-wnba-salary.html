Clark's College Career And WNBA

Caitlin Clark had a record-breaking college career at the University of Iowa.

Widely considered one of the greatest female collegiate players ever, Clark rewrote the Iowa Hawkeyes' record book and the Big Ten conference record book and even etched her name in NCAA (National Collegiate Athletic Association) history.

Recruited by top programs before high school graduation, Clark was a five-star prospect who chose to stay close to home in Iowa.

Clark's freshman year was electric; she averaged an astonishing 26.6 points per game, the highest in all of Division I basketball that year.

These scoring statistics earned her unanimous Big Ten Freshman of the Year honors and helped propel the Hawkeyes to a runner-up finish in the conference tournament.

She shattered freshman program records for points and assists, setting the stage for even more dominance. Clark finished her collegiate career on a high note.

In November 2023, she achieved a milestone by becoming the top scorer in the history of the Hawkeyes basketball program. She also reached an impressive 3,000 career points.

By mid-February 2024, Clark became the highest scorer in NCAA Division I women's basketball history.

Clark made history once more by becoming the highest scorer ever across men's and women's NCAA Division I basketball.

Clark departed Iowa with an impressive record, boasting the highest career points (3,951) and three-pointers (548) in Division I history. She also scored the second-most triple-doubles (17) and the third-most assists (1,144).

Following the season, it was announced that Iowa would retire her jersey number 22, making her the third player in program history to receive the honor.

On February 29, 2024, Clark announced that she would declare for the 2024 WNBA draft. Clark was selected as the first overall pick of the 2024 WNBA draft by the Indiana Fever.

WNBA Salary

Amid the fanfare surrounding Caitlin Clark's arrival in the WNBA, her salary as a rookie with the Indiana Fever presents a contrasting narrative.

The current structure of the WNBA salary system means her initial earnings are significantly lower than her male counterparts in the NBA. Clark's initial WNBA earnings fall short of six figures.

As the first overall draft pick, Clark will receive a starting salary of $76,535 for the 2024 season. This base pay increases modestly over her four-year rookie contract, reaching $97,582 by 2027.

On April 19th, the New York Times covered Caitlin's WNBA contract, and echoed other outlets' assertions that it was shockingly modest for an athlete of her caliber:

"Her starting pay for the Fever — the Women’s National Basketball Association team that selected her as the No. 1 draft pick [in April] — is part of a four-year contract that will total $338,056.

"But to some fans, it still seemed startling low for someone with her star power — especially compared with what her male counterparts in the National Basketball Association make."

Lower draft picks land smaller salaries based on a predetermined scale established in the WNBA's collective bargaining agreement with its players.

It is important to note that this base pay can be supplemented with performance bonuses. For instance, the "Rookie Of The Year" award comes with a $5,150 bonus.

However, even with potential bonuses, Clark's initial WNBA pay still pales in comparison to her counterparts in the NBA.

For reference, Victor Wembanyama, the number one pick in the 2023 NBA Draft, will earn over $12 million in his first year playing for the league.

This disparity in salaries between the WNBA and NBA has sparked renewed debate about fair compensation for professional women athletes in the US, as Vox.com explained:

"The fresh attention for the WNBA draft, however, is also spotlighting the problems the league has had with pay equity. For years, the WNBA’s salaries have lagged the NBA’s by a massive margin.

"That’s due in part to the leagues’ differences in revenue and season lengths. But other factors, like differences in collective bargaining agreements and revenue-sharing, also play a big role."

Caitlin Clark's Endorsements

Caitlin Clark's impact on the basketball court naturally led to lucrative income opportunities off the court.

Even before joining the WNBA, she leveraged her growing popularity and marketability to secure impressively large endorsement deals with major brands.

Notably, one of her earliest endorsement deals was with The Vinyl Studio in August 2021, marking her debut in merchandise endorsements.

"The Queen Of Clapback" inked agreements with major brands like Nike, State Farm, Gatorade, Hy-Vee, Bose, and H&R Block.

Clark earned around $3.1 million before her draft through these deals in connection to her NIL (name, image, and likeness) arrangements.

Although her initial NIL deal, signed in 2022, concluded at the end of the 2023 to 2024 season, Clark remains involved in ten other NIL deals.

Her NIL valuation of $765,000 ranks her third in the College Women's NIL rank.

Caitlin Clark's Nike Deal

Caitlin Clark reportedly inked an eight-year, $28 million contract with the sportswear giant Nike in a groundbreaking move.

The agreement would mark the most lucrative endorsement deal for any female basketball player in history.

As the all-time leading scorer in NCAA Division I history and the top pick in the WNBA draft, Clark's association with Nike extends beyond mere sponsorship, to include the creation of a signature shoe bearing her name.

While competing offers from Under Armour and Adidas offered substantial sums – $16 million over four years and $6 million over four years, respectively – Clark ultimately opted to continue her collaboration with Nike.

The eight-year Nike deal, which was announced on April 18th, will earn her an average of $3.5 million per year, adding a significant amount to Caitlin Clark's net worth when contrasted with her projected WNBA earnings.
